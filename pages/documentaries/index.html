An Incomplete List of Documentaries That I Like

Currently at documentaries.

(click the titles to expand)

The Silent World (1956) - Jacques Cousteau

wikipedia

One of the first documentaries filmed underwater. Cousteau blows up a coral reef and accidentally runs over a whale in his boat.

Streetwise (1984) - Martin Bell

wikipedia

Follows a group of street kids in Seattle. This one's pretty sad.

In the Realms of the Unreal (2004) - Jessica Yu

wikipedia

Henry Darger was an artist who wrote and illustrated really long fantasy books (15,000 pages). His life's work was discovered by his landlord just after his death.

Tokyo-Ga (1985) - Werner Herzog

wikipedia

A film that’s loosely about the director Yasujirô Ozu with extended shots of everyday life in 1980s Tokyo. You should watch this scene to get a feel for it.

Grizzly Man (2005) - Werner Herzog

wikipedia

Timothy Treadwell dedicated his life to bears. Werner walks you through his life and grizzly (sorry) end.

Dear Zachary: A Letter to a Son About His Father (2008) - Kurt Kuenne

wikipedia

This is a sad story about a father, his murderer and their son.

Carts of Darkness (2008) - Murray Siple

wikipedia

Homeless people in Vancouver live off the bottle return scheme. In their spare time they ride down hills in shopping carts really fast.

Cosmos: A Personal Voyage (1980) - Carl Sagan

wikipedia

A documentary series about the universe. This version is way better than the one by Neil deGrasse Tyson.

Sunshine Hotel (2001) - Michael Dominic

wikipedia

A look inside New York's last flophouse.

Angel of Nanjing (2015) - Frank Ferendo, Jordan Horowitz

wikipedia

The Yangtze River Bridge in Nanjing is a suicide hotspot. Chen Si goes down there on his days off and tries to stop people killing themselves.

Tickled (2016) - David Farrier

wikipedia

David Farrier is like a less charismatic Louis Theroux from New Zealand. He investigates a sport called 'Competitive Endurance Tickling' and things get pretty weird.

Arena: A British Guide to the End of the World (2019) - Daniel Vernon

BBC

Cold War in Great Britain. This film shows you the preparations towns were making for when the bomb dropped in London and interviews soldiers who were at the Christmas Island nuclear weapons tests.

Inside Story: Mini (1975) - Franc Roddam

BBC

Mini is an 11 year old kid who compulsively tries to kill his family.

Man on Wire (2008) - James Marsh

wikipedia

A guy tightrope walks between the Twin Towers.

Fourteen Days in May (1987) - Paul Harman

wikipedia

A man's final days on death row before he's executed for a crime he didn't commit.

Crumb (1995) - Terry Zwigoff

wikipedia

Robert Crumb and his family are really weird.

Paris is Burning (1990) - Jennie Livingston

wikipedia

All about the ball scene in 1980s New York.

The Wolfpack (2015) - Crystal Moselle

wikipedia

A family of 7 siblings went 14 years without leaving their parents apartment. For fun they transcribe films and remake them scene for scene with cardboard props.

West of Memphis (2012) - Amy J. Berg

wikipedia

Three teenagers were sentenced to death for the murder of three 8 year old children, supposedly during a satanic ritual. A story about police incompetence and the Satanic Panic of America in the 80s/90s.

The Century of the Self (2002) - Adam Curtis

wikipedia

Examines the origins of consumerism in popular culture and the use of psychoanalysis to sell products.

Bitter Lake (2015) - Adam Curtis

wikipedia

An archive footage documentary about America's relationship with Saudi Arabia and western interference in Afghanistan.

Reverend Death (2008) - Jon Ronson

wikipedia

George Exoo helps with assisted suicides when other organisations won't. Jon Ronson is amazing and did loads of good docs for the BBC in the noughties, many of them are on Youtube.

The Man Whose Mind Exploded (2013) - Toby Amies

wikipedia

A film about Drako Oho Zarharzar who can't form new memories after suffering a head injury. His flat is covered with pieces of paper that he uses to keep track of his life.

The Devil and Daniel Johnston (2005) - Jeff Feuerzeig

wikipedia

Even if you don't like Daniel Johnston this is still worth watching.

The Genius of Photography (2007) - ??

bbc archive.org

You can watch this on the archive.org link. It's pretty niche but one of the most informative and comprehensive documentary series I have ever seen. Give it a go if you're even slightly interested in photography.
